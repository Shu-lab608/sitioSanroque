Sr. San Roque Fiesta Celebration

Every August 20, our community comes alive to honor our patron saint, San Roque, with a week-long celebration blending religious devotion and cultural heritage.

Honoring of Sr. San Roque

San Roque (St. Roch), born around 1295 in Montpellier, France, was a noble who renounced his fortune and became a pilgrim, particularly known for his service to the sick.

The highlight of our fiesta is the grand procession where the image of Sr. San Roque is carried through the streets on a beautifully decorated carriage, followed by devotees holding candles and singing hymns.

The Pandayan (Blacksmiths)

For generations, our community has kept the ancient art of blacksmithing alive, forging tools and weapons with skill passed down through families.

The Blacksmith's Craft

Our blacksmiths, locally known as "Panday," work in small family-owned forges called "Pandayan." Using traditional techniques, they transform raw iron into functional tools, farm implements, and decorative items.

The process begins with heating the metal in a coal-fired forge until it reaches the perfect temperature for shaping. The glowing metal is then hammered on an anvil with precise strikes to form the desired shape.

The Scabbard Craft

Discover the art of crafting scabbards, a vital part of traditional weapons. Skilled artisans use time-honored techniques to create durable and beautifully crafted scabbards that complement the blades they protect.

The Craft of Scabbard Making

Scabbard crafting is a meticulous art, requiring precise measurements and an understanding of both the materials and tools. The process involves shaping the wood, adding decorative elements, and ensuring a secure fit for the blade.

From leather to wood, every scabbard reflects the craftsmanship of its maker, offering both protection and aesthetic beauty for the blade it holds.

The Paraglukad

Meet our coconut farmers locally known as "Paraglukad" who brave the heat, climb great heights, and masterfully process coconuts into valuable products that support entire families and communities.

Coconut Farming Life

From harvesting coconuts high up the trees to sun-drying copra for days under the sun, every step is a labor of dedication. Our farmers use traditional tools, carry heavy loads, and work long hours rain or shine.

Despite the hardships, "Paraglukad" take pride in their heritage. Their craft not only sustains their families but also preserves a vital part of our rural economy.

Shell Craft Tradition

Crafting starts from collecting shells by the shore, followed by washing and drying. With tools as simple as needles and pliers, they drill, carve, and assemble intricate designs.

This heritage craft symbolizes our connection to the sea each piece a story of patience, skill, and tradition.
